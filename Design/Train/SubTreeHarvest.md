Denote f(x_t) is the prior generated by the neural network, and f^n(x_t) is the refined value with n visits.
The current pipeline trains f(x_t) to match f^n(x_t), which wastes subtrees.
A child of x_t can be denoted as x_t^p, where p denotes the path down the tree.
We can also train f(x_t^p) to match f^m(x_t^p)

To solve problems, we need a variety of gating mechanisms
    Noisy target: min-visit gate
    Selection bias(over representation/near duplicates...): entropy gate
    Drift: Pray anchored moves anchor drift enough(should be fine, I expect subtree harvesting to increase training targets less than double)
